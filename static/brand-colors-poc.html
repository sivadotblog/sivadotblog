Design Rationale

Evidence-Based Color Decisions

  • Background Main: Maximizes polarity while softening extremes for comfort
  • Card/Article Background: Maintains separation of content blocks in both modes
  • Body Text Primary: High contrast with reduced glare vs pure black/white
  • Secondary/Meta Text: Clear hierarchy without undermining legibility
  • Primary Accent: Green tones shown to be less fatiguing than saturated reds on dark backgrounds
  • Secondary Accent: Depth for headings and UI states across themes
  • Plum Highlight: High-contrast highlight used sparingly for hierarchy, not body text

Accessibility & Usage Guidelines

Contrast Ratios

All text combinations meet WCAG AA standards for contrast ratios, ensuring readability for users with visual impairments.

Green Advantage

Green primary accent reduces eye fatigue in dark mode compared to warm colors like red or orange, which can feel harsh against dark backgrounds.

Plum Moderation

The plum highlight should be used sparingly - ideal for callouts, badges, and special emphasis, but avoid large text blocks.
